\section{Upper-Level Substitution Bias}
\label{app:substitution-bias}

The Boskin Commission (1996) emphasized that fixed-basket price indexes may
overstate changes in the cost of living when they do not account for
substitution across products or categories after relative price changes. This
appendix applies this insight to our distributional setting by asking whether
the measured inflation gaps across income groups are sensitive to the
upper-level aggregation formula used to combine COICOP price relatives. We
compare the baseline group-specific HICP aggregation with a chained Tornqvist
alternative that uses information from adjacent expenditure shares. The exercise
does not model within-category variety substitution, but provides a diagnostic
for the role of upper-level substitution in measured inflation inequality.


Let \(p_{j,y,m}\) denote the HICP price index of COICOP category \(j\) in
month \(m\) of year \(y\), and let \(p_{j,y,0}\) denote the price reference
used for the HICP weight year \(y\), as in the main-text price-index
notation. For household group \(g\), let \(\omega_{j,g,y}\) be the annual
group-specific HICP weight assigned to COICOP category \(j\) in year \(y\).
These weights are obtained by combining official HICP item weights with HBS
expenditure shares by income group.

For each household group \(g\), the baseline calculation first computes the
Laspeyres-type within-year aggregate \(P^L_{g,y,m}\) using the annual weights
available for the corresponding link period:

\[
P^L_{g,y,m}
=
\sum_{j \in \mathcal{J}_y}
\omega_{j,g,y}
\cdot
\frac{p_{j,y,m}}{p_{j,y,0}},
\]

where \(\mathcal{J}_y\) is the set of COICOP categories available in weight
year \(y\). The chained Laspeyres-type group index \(I^L_{g,y,m}\) is then
obtained by linking \(P^L_{g,y,m}\) to the previous December.

The comparison index uses the Tornqvist formula at the same upper level. Let
\(\omega_{j,g,y-1}\) and \(\omega_{j,g,y}\) denote the previous-year and
current-year group-specific weights used for the link. The within-year
Tornqvist aggregate is:

\[
P^T_{g,y,m}
=
\prod_{j \in \mathcal{J}_y}
\left(
\frac{p_{j,y,m}}{p_{j,y,0}}
\right)^{
\frac{1}{2}
\left(
\omega_{j,g,y-1} + \omega_{j,g,y}
\right)
}.
\]

The chained Tornqvist group index \(I^T_{g,y,m}\) is obtained by applying the
same annual chaining convention as for \(I^L_{g,y,m}\).

For each group \(g\), the comparison is made using annualized growth rates over
the selected sample window. If \((y_0,m_0)\) and \((y_1,m_1)\) are the first
and last months available in the comparison, and \(n\) is the number of elapsed
months, the annualized growth rate of chained index \(I\) is:

\[
\gamma_g(I)
= \left[
\left(
\frac{I_{g,y_1,m_1}}{I_{g,y_0,m_0}}
\right)^{12/n} - 1
\right] \times 100.
\]

The upper-level substitution-bias diagnostic is then defined as:

\[
\operatorname{SB}_g
=
\gamma_g(I^L)
-
\gamma_g(I^T).
\]

A positive value indicates that the chained Laspeyres aggregation grows faster
than the chained Tornqvist alternative over the same period.

Table~\ref{tab:fr-substitution-bias} reports the diagnostic for France by
income quintile, using the national-source French level-3 workflow from 2019
onwards. Compared with the level-2 calculation, the results are very close in
aggregate but the quintile-specific substitution-bias estimates are slightly
larger, especially for the first three income quintiles.

The estimates in Table~\ref{tab:fr-substitution-bias} are small but positive
for all income groups. The difference is somewhat larger for the first income quintile than for the fifth quintile in
this example, suggesting that the choice of upper-level formula can affect the
measured distributional profile of inflation, even when the magnitude remains
modest.
